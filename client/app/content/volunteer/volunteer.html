Volunteer Opportunities

Why should I volunteer with Kids Count? Because...

  1. Fewer than 50% of Alachua County low-income students who entered 9th grade five years ago will graduate this year.
  2. Rawlings and Williams are two out of of five Alachua County elementary schools with over 85% of students coming from low-income families.
  3. Research has shown that if a child falls behind in reading as early as first grade, without intervention, there is little chance he will ever catch up with his peers.
  4. Standardized test scores of third grade students are used to predict future prison population.
  5. We commit to work passionately to provide the intervention needed to help our students succeed.
  6. We all lose when children don't grow and develop into all they can be.
  7. Kids Count is research based.
  8. We provide students one-on-one literacy tutoring with trained tutors.
  9. We work with the whole child to inspire a love of life and learning in our students through character development, education and special enrichment activities.

What are the steps to volunteer?

  1. Contact us by clicking on the Volunteer Link at the top of the page.
  2. Attend an Orientation.
  3. Undergo a background check.
  4. Start working with our kids!

Links to our Volunteer Application and our Volunteer Handbook (that we will go over during Orientation) are located to the left. There is no need to fill them out right now but they will provide some useful information to get you started.
